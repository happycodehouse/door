OUR STORY

  • 1979 - 1991
  • 1995 - 2010
  • 2011 - Present
1979 - 1991
  • 1979

    George Lucas recruits Ed Catmull
    to head Lucasfilm's Computer Division,
    marking the beginning of computer technology development for the film industry.

  • 1986

    Steve Jobs purchases the Computer Division,
    establishing Pixar as an independent company,
    and collaboration between Pixar and Disney begins.

  • 1988

    "Tin Toy" wins the Academy Award for Best Short Film (Animated),
    becoming the first computer-animated film to receive an Oscar.

  • 1991

    Pixar announces an agreement with Disney
    to make and distribute at least one computer-generated animated movie,
    starting work on "Toy Story."

1995 - 2010
  • 1995

    "Toy Story," the first computer-animated feature film,
    is released and becomes the highest-grossing film of the year.

  • 1996

    "Toy Story" receives Academy Award nominations,
    marking a significant recognition for animated films.

  • 1998

    "A Bug's Life" is released,
    and groundbreaking for Pixar’s Emeryville studio begins.

  • 2001

    "Monsters, Inc." is released,
    further solidifying Pixar's position in the animation industry.

  • 2006

    The Walt Disney Company announces its acquisition of Pixar Animation Studios,
    marking a major shift in the animation landscape.

  • 2010

    "Toy Story 3" is released,
    continuing the legacy of the franchise and showcasing Pixar's ongoing success.

2011 - Present
  • 2011

    "Cars 2" is released,
    celebrating Pixar's 25th anniversary.

  • 2012

    "Brave" is released,
    and Pixar's long-lived animation system Menv is officially retired
    in favor of the new system, Presto.

  • 2015

    "Inside Out" premieres at the Cannes Film Festival
    and achieves great success.

  • 2017

    "Coco" is released in North America
    and becomes Mexico's highest-grossing film of all time.

  • 2020

    "Soul" is released on Disney+,
    continuing Pixar's innovation in animation.

  • 2024

    "Inside Out 2" opens in theaters
    and becomes the highest-grossing animated film of all time.
